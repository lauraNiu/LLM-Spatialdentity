#  LLM-assisted qualitative analysis prompt 

## LLM semi-structured interview guide

| Prompt                       | Description                                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Spatial  Functions           | What are the  spatial characteristics that influence the satisfaction of Hong Kong citizens  in their perception of Shenzhen’s cross-border spaces? |
| Temporal Events              | How do temporal  events (e.g., opening of new transport systems, holidays) affect the spatial  satisfaction of Hong Kong residents regarding Shenzhen’s cross-border spaces? |
| Sentiment Nature             | What sentiments  (positive, negative, neutral) are commonly expressed in the social media  posts, and how do these relate to the spatial satisfaction of Hong Kong  residents towards Shenzhen? |
| Transport and  Accessibility | How do  transportation systems (e.g., high-speed rail, metro) influence spatial  satisfaction? What improvements are seen as necessary to enhance convenience? |
| Causal Factors               | What are the  main factors causing either positive or negative spatial satisfaction based  on the posts reviewed? How do social, economic, or cultural aspects  contribute? |

 

## LLM-powered inference prompt strategy

| Prompt                   | Description                                                  |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Role-Playing Prompt      | The LLM is instructed to act  as a senior urban planner in order to generate insights relevant to spatial  identity. |
| Driving Factors Analysis | Based on the input, the LLM  identifies the primary drivers of spatial identity, such as accessibility,  comfort, and amenities. |
| Iterative Clarification  | Ambiguous or unexpected  responses from the model are clarified through iterative questioning, to  ensure accuracy in the attribution of causes. |
| Optimization Suggestions | The LLM suggests spatial  optimizations that might enhance the satisfaction of Hong Kong residents,  such as improving infrastructure or enhancing service quality at border  crossings. |
| Emotion-Driven Causes    | The model identifies  emotional drivers behind positive or negative spatial satisfaction, and  suggests methods to address these emotional needs in urban planning. |
